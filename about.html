About the Island

Nestled in the Pacific, Taniti is a small, remote tropical island covering less than 500 square miles. Despite its modest size, the island offers a remarkable variety of landscapes, including sandy and rocky beaches, a safe natural harbor, vibrant rainforests, and a mountainous interior with an active volcano ideal for hiking. Taniti is home to around 20,000 indigenous people and was, until recently, primarily supported by fishing and agriculture.
